Cricket 101

New to cricket? Here's everything you need to understand this project.

🏏

What is Cricket?

Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of 11. One team bats, trying to score "runs," while the other bowls and fields, trying to take "wickets" (dismissals). The team with the most runs wins.

🏆

What is the IPL?

The Indian Premier League is the world's richest cricket league. Launched in 2008, it features 10 city-based franchises playing Twenty20 (T20) cricket — the shortest, most explosive format, with each team facing just 20 overs (120 balls).

📊

How Playoffs Work

The top 4 teams from the group stage qualify. Qualifier 1 (1st vs 2nd) gives both teams two chances. The Eliminator (3rd vs 4th) is sudden death. Qualifier 2 produces the second finalist. The Final crowns the champion.

🤖

What This Model Does

It trains 6 ML algorithms on historical IPL data, engineers features like team strength, venue bias, head-to-head records, and toss impact, then runs 50,000 Monte Carlo simulations to estimate each team's championship probability.

📖 Key Terms Glossary

OverA set of 6 legal deliveries bowled by one bowler. T20 = 20 overs per innings.
WicketA dismissal — getting a batter out. Each team has 10 wickets per innings.
NRRNet Run Rate — average runs scored per over minus average runs conceded. Used as a tiebreaker.
TossA coin flip before the match. The winner chooses to bat first (set a target) or field first (chase).
Strike RateRuns scored per 100 balls faced. Higher = more aggressive batter.
EconomyRuns conceded per over by a bowler. Lower = more economical (better).
PowerplayThe first 6 overs where fielding restrictions apply, usually producing the highest scoring rate.
Monte CarloA simulation technique that runs thousands of random scenarios to estimate outcome probabilities.

Project Overview

About This Project

Built by Sahas Reddy Etikyala — a showcase of applied machine learning, data engineering, and sports analytics.

📡

Live Data Pipeline

Pulls real-time match data from Cricsheet.org — updated within 24 hours of each match.

⚙️

Feature Engineering

11 engineered features: team strength, NRR, h2h win rate, toss impact, venue bias, batting/bowling indexes.

🤖

Ensemble ML

Weighted ensemble of Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, XGBoost, CatBoost, and LightGBM.

🎲

Monte Carlo Simulator

50,000 tournament simulations using vectorized NumPy sampling to generate robust championship probabilities.

📊

Interpretable Predictions

Every prediction includes per-model votes, feature importance, NL commentary, and a confidence tier label.

🔗

Shareable Links

Encode team/venue/toss in the URL — share any prediction directly with friends or teammates.
